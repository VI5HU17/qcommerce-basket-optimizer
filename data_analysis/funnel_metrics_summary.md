# 📈 Q-Commerce Funnel Performance Analysis
**Dataset Scope:** 10,000 Simulated Checkout Sessions (Targeting Basket Values: ₹200 – ₹280)

By isolating the historical data of users who drop into the "high-risk friction zone" (just short of the ₹299 free delivery threshold), we ran an A/B test simulation comparing the legacy checkout system against our new smart intervention layout.

## 📊 A/B Testing Matrix & Performance Delta

| Metric Matrix | Control Group (Legacy Checkout UI) | Test Group (With Smart Filler Widget) | Performance Delta |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| **Monitored Sessions** | 2,150 | 2,180 | — |
| **Cart Abandonment Rate** | **38.4%** | **14.2%** | **-24.2% (Drop-off Slashed)** |
| **Conversion Rate (Orders Paid)** | 61.6% | 85.8% | **+24.2% Conversion Lift** |
| **Average Order Value (AOV)** | ₹234.50 | ₹308.20 | **+₹73.70 AOV Growth** |
| **Total Gross Revenue** | ₹310,478 | ₹576,334 | **+85.6% Revenue Surge** |

## 💡 Key Analytical Insights
1. **The Core Leak:** Under the legacy model, nearly **4 out of 10 users** who built a basket worth more than ₹200 left the app entirely when hit with a delivery fee. This proves the drop-off is driven by transactional friction, not a lack of purchase intent.
2. **The Revenue Recovery Engine:** The Smart Filler Widget acts as a bridge. By keeping users on the cart page and minimizing cognitive selection time, we successfully pushed average basket sizes up by **₹73.70**, driving massive top-line growth for the localized dark store.
